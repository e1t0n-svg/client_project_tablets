Talent Asessments and Pre-Hire Tests

Career Development Offfice

Some employers, especially those who receive a large amount of applications, utilize “Talent Assessments” or other similarly named pre-hire methods of testing potential candidates. Pre-assessments are becoming more common in the recruiting process to reduce bias in the hiring process. Navigating these assessments can be challenging at first - this guide provides an overview of pre-hire testing and additional resources to make this process less overwhelming. What are talent assessments/tests, and why do employers use them? Talent assessments, application assessments, pre-employment tests, talent tests, application tests, etc. are names for assessments utilized by employers as part of the application process. These assessments are often utilized in conjunction with ATS (application tracking systems) software, which screens applicants based on certain keywords in their application materials as they reflect elements of the job description. Employers use them as a way to filter applicants based on their compatibility with the company and the position - they can be used to assess personality, skills, work style, industry knowledge, etc. These assessments can be used to demonstrate a candidate’s technical skills, as well as assess if their personality fits the company. How do employers create talent assessments? Employers, especially large companies who may expect several hundred applicants for a position, use data compiled from ATS systems and successful employees in the past to determine which traits or skills to test for, which they feel will determine if an employee can succeed in the position. Employers, especially very large companies, will create very specific job descriptions and test for applicants whose skills or personality best align with that description.
